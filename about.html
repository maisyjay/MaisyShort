ABOUT

My practice as a fashion photographer is grounded in black and white imagery, exploring the ongoing tension between analogue and digital production. I am drawn to the visual language of early 20th century photography; exploring lighting, composition and texture to craft images. I use post-production to question how digital tools can re imagine 1920s photography. In my video work ‘Galons’ I use sound scape to emanate the technological process of fustian production in Hebden Bridge, blending my personal heritage with my digital interests. Alongside my photographic practice, I work across creative direction, sound engineering, animation and 3D software. My collective video project squirm explored post-production to create a feeling of auditory and visual discomfort. Working across these areas has shaped my understanding of image-making as a collaborative process between disciplines, where each medium contributes to narrative. This has developed my interest in how creative coding can extend my concepts and outcomes, and how interactivity can sit within my work.
